The Residence Hall Association strives to create a welcoming and safe living environment in which residents can expect to be treated equally and respectfully. Learn more
Our structure is set up in a way that each person is in charge of a certain area of the organization. This allows for people to be able to have autonomy within their position while still communicating with the rest of the team. Even though each executive board member is in charge of their own area, we all work as a team to get projects and initiatives done. Learn more
We try to have a variety of different programs for residents. Some are socially focused while others are more educationally focused. We decide on what programs we will do based on what we hear from residents and other student leaders. Each term we strive to put on 6-8 different programs. During the winter and spring term, we also put on a co-program with all three councils. Each executive board member is in charge of putting together at least one program a term. Learn more

Portland State is Pretty Great

Here at Portland State University, we are a community, we are students, we are Vikings Portland State University is a campus created by the city, for the city. Portland State is unlike your average University. Portland State was born out of a need for knowledge and a place for community after the old campus was flooded back in 1948. Portland State University wasn’t built from scratch, rather then it was created by the beautiful historical buildings that PSU bought, over time buildings have been built, modernized, students have been enrolling, the funky and eclectic little university grew from one building to over 50 buildings that are affiliated with Portland State. One of the most exciting things about life here at PSU is living on campus and being enriched in everything the city has to offer. Portland State University has 9 different residence halls that offer a wide variety of room options to fit the needs of the different groups of residents we have living here on campus. For example, PSU offers first year floors with all first year students that are taking the same classes, quad units for people who need a little more room, single units for residents who need to live alone, family units for residents that have families, and many other options to fit anyone’s needs. Living, working, and going to school at Portland State University is such a uniquely amazing experience. Living in the heart of downtown Portland, there is an unlimited supply of opportunities, explorations, and friends to make. From everyone at the Residence Hall Association here at PSU, thank you for taking the time to get to know us and our wonderful institution. Let knowledge serve the city and let knowledge serve the PACURH region.
